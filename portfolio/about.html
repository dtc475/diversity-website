About


I started out like most people do: snoring through third period french. I wandered for a while before a great personal loss drove me back into my education, where I re-discovered a passion that I'd always had; creation. In 2013 I was hired at a publishing magazine as a Fiction Editor. One thing led to another and six months into this job and I was smack dab in the middle of Rome, Italy.


I know, right. To be honest it came as kind of a surprise to me, as well.


Two months, a shattered iPhone 4s (thank you St. Petersberg cobblestone streets), and blistered feet later I was back in the states. I was tired, sunburnt, happy to be able to do a simple load of laundry again, but that two months of hostels and overnight trains with tiny compartments taught me something: the sunrise waits for no-one.


I was accepted into Washington State University: Vancouver in early 2014, and began attending that fall, as a Digital Technology and Culture Major, in the Creative Media and Digital Culture department. My specialty (we call them superpowers, cause we're neat like that) swings between graphic design and web development, because I enjoy both. I'm also in the process of getting my Professional Writing certificate.
