Professional Writing


I've been writing since my parents bought me a 1960s typewriter from a garage sale down the street. I was probably all of six at the time, mashing on buttons to make awesome noises. My parents would pretend to read what I had written like you pretend with any six year old.


It became a sort of habit. Got me in trouble a few times, too, as most habits do.


In 2011, I started writing again. At the behest of my professor, I submitted my short fiction story, Alabaster, to Clark College's The Phoenix Magazine, and to my surprise, my submission was accepted. It remains to this day, possibly the proudest moment of my life. I may or may not have stood in my kitchen with the acceptance email sobbing. But you'll never be able to prove it.


Since then, I've continued working on my writing skills, both in a professional and fictional sense. I'm working on my professional writing certificate, as well as an English Minor from WSUV.
